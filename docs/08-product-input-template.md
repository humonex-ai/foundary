# Product Input — Template

The **Product Input** is the canonical starting point for every Foundry project.
A founder fills it before any artifact is generated; it is the head of the
artifact chain and the Vision agent's input:

```
Product Input → Vision → Architecture → Roadmap → Work Orders
```

Keep it to roughly one page. If it is longer than the Vision it produces, it is
trying to do the Vision's job. The goal is enough structure to remove ambiguity —
not a specification. A filled copy lives at `projects/<name>/product-input.md`.

---

## Problem

What hurts today. One paragraph, concrete, not aspirational. The pain that
justifies building anything at all.

## Users

Who has this problem. Primary user first. Be specific about role and context, not
"everyone."

## Goals

What success enables. 3–5 outcomes, not features. Phrase as results, not
implementation.

## Non-Goals

What this explicitly will **not** do. Scope discipline starts here — the clearest
single defense against scope creep.

## Constraints

Hard limits: technology, budget, timeline, team size, regulatory, existing
systems that must be respected.

## Success Criteria

How we will know it worked. Measurable where possible.

## Assumptions

What we are taking as true but have not verified. Surfacing these makes
garbage-in visible and makes later drift detection possible — when an assumption
breaks, you know which artifacts to re-check.

## Open Questions

Known unknowns the founder has not resolved yet. Prevents false confidence by
recording what is still undecided.
